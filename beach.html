Beaches You Can't Visit In Colorado

Aren't beaches great? We think so too! The best part about them is the smell, isn't it? Well, if you're looking to get some brine in your life, then look no further than anywhere else other than the Centennial State.

Venice Beach, SoCal

Ah, Venice Beach. Tourism at its finest. This beach stands out for its large ferris wheel and carnival atmosphere, making it a perfect place to bring the family to experience the pinnacle of SoCal toursim. Surf, swim, or tan all year round at this temperate paridise just west of downtown Los Angeles. While Colorado sure does offer a lot, it sure doesn't offer all of that! But hey, at least we have mountains.

Jersey Shore, New Jersey

If you think of the reality tv show when you hear the words Jersey Shore, then prepare to have your expecations inverted. While this beachtown does have a decent nightlife, you'll find that its pleasant summer breeze and beautiful sunrises will make Jersey Shore a sunny vacation that the family is sure not to forget. Too bad we don't have one of these in Colorado, huh? We'll keep our arid climate and freak yearround snowstorms, thank you very much.

Sunset Beach, North Carolina

North Carolina is nearly a tropical paradise in the spring months, but without the requirements of long international plane rides and passports. When looking for a family vacation, you shouldn't look any further than the sunny beaches of Sunset Beach. What does Colorado have to offer? Why, we have wildcats and legal weed! What more could you ever want?
